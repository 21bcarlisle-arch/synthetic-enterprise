# Phase 1c Summary — Forward Curve Pricing

## What Was Built
- `sim/forward_curve.py` — `generate_forward_price()`: a synthetic forward price (Law 3) built from real historical SSP spot data, with three independently-configurable components — a 90-day rolling base average, a sigma-based volatility premium scaled by a `risk_factor` (default 1.2), and a seasonal adjustment (winter/summer multipliers, blended across the months the contract delivers into)
- `saas/tariff_pricing.py` — `price_fixed_tariff()` refactored from a self-contained 30-day-spot-lookback pricer into a small, pure margin-application step (`forward_price * (1 + margin)`), moving curve-generation onto the SIM side of the seam per the brief's constraint
- `simulation/run_phase1c.py` — re-runs the exact 2016 settlement Phase 0c ran, with only the pricing methodology swapped, for a clean apples-to-apples comparison
- `simulation/run_phase1c_full_window.py` — runs the same pipeline across the entire simulation window (2016-01-01 → 2025-06-07) and additionally breaks portfolio P&L down by calendar year

## Key Findings
- **The forward curve is a dramatic, structural fix for the 2016 losses.** Phase 0c's 30-day-lookback pricing produced revenue £365.71 / cost £443.99 / margin **−£78.28**; identical customers, identical consumption, identical real wholesale data, repriced via the forward curve produced revenue £942.67 / cost £443.99 / margin **+£498.68** — a swing of +£576.96, flipping the book from loss to a ~53% margin. The fix worked exactly as theorised: loading in a sigma-based volatility premium and a winter-weighted seasonal blend (rather than a flat backward-looking spot average) is what closes the systematic under-pricing gap a real forward desk would also have to close.
- **Customer dissatisfaction fell roughly 4-25x in lockstep.** Phase 0c saw ~25% of all periods trigger the "wholesale cost > 120% of bill" reaction (e.g. C1: 4,131/17,517); under forward-curve pricing this fell to roughly 1-7% (C1: 174/17,517, C2: 1,047/17,518, C3: 1,218/17,518, C4: 851/17,518) — directly consistent with bills now actually covering the cost of supply most of the time. Pricing methodology and the Experience-observability layer are visibly entangled, exactly as the Key Domain Insight predicts.
- **The full-window run surfaced a real architectural gap, not a pricing result.** Running 2016-01-01 → 2025-06-07 produced settlement records for **2016 and 2017 only** — nothing for 2018 through mid-2025. `simulation/settlement.py`'s existing `CONTRACT_LENGTH_DAYS = 365` rule is doing exactly what it's documented to do: each of the four customers is active for exactly one year from acquisition (2016-01-01/04-01/07-01/10-01), and by 2017-10-01 every contract has expired with **no renewal or new-acquisition mechanic to replace it**. 165,386 real SSP records were fetched for the full 9.5-year window — over seven years' worth never touched by settlement, because the book empties out after ~21 months. This simulation currently models a single cohort's single contract term, not a living book of business.

## Key Decisions Made
- Kept curve-generation entirely on the SIM side of the seam (per the brief's explicit constraint) — `saas/tariff_pricing.py` now receives a plain `forward_price: float` and does nothing but apply the business margin, which keeps the Point-in-Time Blindfold structural and Law 3's "synthetic, clearly labelled" requirement easy to audit at a single point
- Made `risk_factor` and the seasonal multipliers named module-level constants in `sim/forward_curve.py` (`WINTER_MONTHS`/`WINTER_MULTIPLIER`/`SUMMER_MULTIPLIER`) rather than inlining them, satisfying "risk factor and seasonal adjustments are configurable parameters" and making future tuning a one-line change
- Blended the seasonal multiplier across every month the `contract_length_months`-month delivery period spans (not just the acquisition month) — this is what makes `contract_length_months` a meaningful input to the pricing function rather than a decorative parameter, and reflects how a real fixed-rate forward price has to account for the whole period it covers
- Chose to **run the existing pipeline honestly and report what it actually does** for the full-window deliverable, rather than inventing a contract-renewal or customer-growth mechanic mid-phase to produce a "nicer-looking" 9.5-year P&L — that decision belongs to Rich, not to a delegation round buried inside a review-gated phase

## Open Questions
- **How should the book grow to fill the simulation window?** Two distinct mechanics could close the 2018-2025 gap — contract renewal (existing customers re-price at the then-current forward curve when their term ends) and/or new customer acquisition (a growing cohort over time, which would also start feeding the CLV/CAC observability layer CLAUDE.md calls for). These are materially different modelling choices with different knock-on effects on CLV, churn, and hedge-effectiveness — worth Rich's steer before either is built
- Should `tools/delegate_ollama.py` get a post-processing step to strip markdown fences and detect/relocate misplaced docstrings automatically? Both defects are now well-established repeat patterns (fence-wrap: 4/4 again this phase; docstring placement: correctly landed once this phase when the prompt explicitly said "before any import, not after" — suggesting the fix is in the prompting, not the harness)

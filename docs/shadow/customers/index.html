The Divergence

The SIM knows C7 had a real life event (new baby on 2023-12-23) that pushed income stress from LOW to MODERATE from 2023 onward. A real supplier cannot see either fact directly -- the company only ever observes the downstream payment behaviour: score FAIR, on-time rate 74.6%, DD-fail rate 9.6%, and 6 real arrears cases opening from the same period. That gap -- true cause invisible, only the payment-behaviour symptom observable -- is the basis risk this retrofit makes visible.

The Divergence

C_IC3 is one real dual-fuel account, but its electricity and gas legs are billed, paid, and chased for arrears completely separately -- electricity has 0 failed payment(s) and 0 arrears case(s), while gas has 1 and 1 respectively, with a gas balance of -£89,641. A combined roll-up nets these into one number and hides which fuel is actually causing the friction -- this is why per-fuel legs must be a real, separate view, not just an internal computation the combined total is derived from.

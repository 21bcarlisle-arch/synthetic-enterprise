## Why it matters (keep the framing)
Adoption of ToU, control and DER services is a JOURNEY, not a switch — and
forward of today it is where the industry's actual battle is. It is also the
missing precondition for ToU tariffs (a preference model must exist before
time-varying prices mean anything behaviourally).

## Scope as decided (record verbatim in the registration)
- **Hidden adoption physics behind the wall.** Each customer carries hidden
  traits governing conversion: a being-bothered threshold (for many, the £ at
  stake sits BELOW it — this must be honestly true in the population, not
  tuned away), friction sensitivity, trust/reassurance needs, reward
  responsiveness. Extends the engagement archetypes; consumes the deferred
  NUDGE_PHYSICS mechanisms (present bias, friction costs, defaults, social
  norms, commitment devices) with the same behavioural-science anchoring
  discipline as the shipped framing/tone work.
- **Key moments as conversion windows.** Adoption is state- and
  event-dependent: EV purchase, house move, boiler failure, smart-meter
  install, bill shock, renewal. These are events in the SIM's event stream;
  the company's job is DETECTING/INFERRING the moment through the wall and
  acting within the window.
- **Incentives and loyalty integrated into billing.** Points, rewards, free
  hours, half-price hours, miles, prizes are becoming standard ToU/EV
  mechanics. They are bill-visible artefacts (statement lines, reward
  balances, discounted-window tariff structures), NOT marketing copy — they
  touch the billing lane and the bill artefact standard, and their cost is a
  real P&L line. Where flexibility value per customer sits below the bother
  threshold, engagement/loyalty mechanics carry the persuasion load — and the
  company must DISCOVER that rather than assume rational £-response.

## Non-negotiables
- Adoption rate is a DIAGNOSTIC, never a target (Law A). No tuning hidden
  susceptibility to hit an adoption number.
- **Advisor amendment (director-approved):** adoption susceptibility
  DISTRIBUTIONS are BASELINE-world fidelity — anchored to real ToU/smart-tariff
  uptake data (validator anchors), never tuned (Law B). The INCENTIVE MECHANICS
  the company offers are supplier-side strategy it must learn. Keep that line
  clean.
- Nudge mechanics stay two-sided under Consumer Duty: gamified incentives and
  pressure at key moments have mis-selling/vulnerability edges the compliance
  organs must see.
- The wall holds: the company observes offers, responses and behaviour —
  never the hidden thresholds.

## The ruling: W1_8 zonal pricing is closed

He is right on the facts: the government **rejected zonal pricing in July 2025** and committed to reformed national pricing. W1_8 models a future that was explicitly declined. Close the atom (retire or reduce to a one-line watching brief in the register below — your call which). The earlier console line opening W1_8 DISCOVER is superseded by this ruling.

**The principle to record:** researching *contingent* futures — policy that may never happen — spends fidelity budget on counterfactual worlds. Thinking ahead is valuable only when ranked by **certainty × mission-criticality**:
- **Certain futures** (EV/heat-pump growth, smart-meter completion, warming winters) — every possible world contains them. W1_10 was this class, done right.
- **Committed futures** (legislated, dated — e.g. market-wide half-hourly settlement) — live programme, not debate.
- **Mission-required capabilities** — things the ratified purpose cannot function without.
- **Contingent policy** (zonal, further cap reform shapes) — watching brief only, one line, never a build.

## The forward-discovery register (new, standing)

Establish a small register of DISCOVER-only forward tracks, run under the **optional/preemptible class** from the resource-aware scheduling design — they soak spare capacity and yield instantly to core work, so thinking-ahead never competes with building. Five inaugural tracks, ranked:

**F1 — Simulating conversations (highest).** The pitch's core claim is that tone, timing and channel ARE the abatement mechanism — yet the sim has no conversations: customers do not respond to messages; the company does not generate them. Both sides need designing. DISCOVER: what real UK supplier↔customer conversation looks like (contact rates, complaint taxonomies, CSS/Ofgem benchmarks, tone-of-voice evidence, channel mix by segment); how message→behaviour response could be modelled against the engagement axes already estimated; what the epistemic wall means here (the company sees replies, never true intent). This also directly serves the segmentation work: channel preference and trust are conversation-revealed traits.

**F2 — Explaining what we do, simply.** Comprehension is a legal duty (consent that was not understood is not consent) and a trust mechanism, not a nicety. DISCOVER: plain-language standards (CMA/FCA Consumer Duty comprehension testing practice, energy-sector precedents); a testable bar — can a lay reader say back what Poesys does with their data and money; where the current site/pitch fails that bar. Feeds the volunteer programme and the site.

**F3 — Volunteer programme mechanics.** The pitch makes "will not open until a security posture review has completed" a public gate — no atom exists behind it. DISCOVER: what that review must cover; consent and data-handling design (GDPR basis, revocation, minimisation); what the smallest honest volunteer pilot looks like. This is the critical path to any real household.

**F4 — International expansion probe.** The pitch asserts "global by design" and §13 admits transfer is unproven. DISCOVER: pick ONE candidate second market; enumerate concretely what varies (market structure, settlement, tariff regulation, data availability, consumer protection); assess which variations the architecture's parameters absorb versus which break assumptions. Output = evidence for or against the transferability claim, not a market-entry plan.

**F5 — Simulated competitor field.** Already flagged as an Epoch-2 gap: pricing decisions currently face no opposition. DISCOVER: minimum competitor model that makes pricing/retention meaningful (tariff-following vs strategic entrants), anchored to real switching-market behaviour.

Register format: one file, one section per track, each with purpose, current findings, and a **certainty × criticality** tag. Contingent-policy items (zonal, etc.) live at the bottom as one-line watching briefs. Add tracks over time on the same test; the director invites additions he has not thought of.

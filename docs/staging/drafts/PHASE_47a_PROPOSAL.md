# Phase 47a: Ofgem Domestic Price Cap

## Status: PROPOSED (auto-loop, 2026-06-23)

## Problem
CSS data from EDF/British Gas confirms domestic supply was loss-making 2019-2023:
- Cap era (2019-2022): sector ~-£4bn cumulative EBIT
- EDF 2023 (recovery): dom elec +4.2%, dom gas -6.1%
- EDF 2024: dom elec +5.4%, dom gas -5.4% (gas still loss-making)

Our simulation shows resi/elec at 10.2% and resi/gas at 11.1% — impossible post-2019.
Root cause: company sets unit_rate = company_fwd × (1 + premium), unconstrained.
In reality, Ofgem's Default Tariff Cap limits what suppliers can charge SVT/default tariff customers.

## Design (simplified — ballpark, not quarterly precision)

Rich confirmed: "not after perfect year on year matching, just the right ball park."

**Approach**: Annual cap lookup table (£/MWh unit rate equivalent) by fuel, applied when:
- Customer segment = "resi" (residential)
- Tariff type = "fixed" on SVT (standard variable) OR any non-notified renewal

**Cap levels (simplified, electricity unit rate equivalent)**:
| Period | Cap approx (elec unit rate) | Source |
|--------|---------------------------|--------|
| 2016-2018 | No cap (competitive market) | Pre-cap era |

| 2020 | ~£0.157/kWh | Ofgem |
| 2021 | ~£0.171/kWh (raised Oct) | Ofgem |
| 2022 | £0.28–0.34/kWh (energy crisis) | Ofgem EPG |
| 2023 | £0.30/kWh (EPG then back to cap) | Ofgem |
| 2024 | £0.22–0.24/kWh (normalising) | Ofgem |

Gas cap: ~40-50% of electricity level in £/MWh equivalent.

**Implementation**:
1. `company/pricing/ofgem_price_cap.py` — lookup function `get_cap_unit_rate(fuel, year)` returning £/kWh
2. `company/pricing/tariff_engine.py` — `price_fixed_tariff()` for resi customers: clamp `unit_rate = min(unit_rate, cap_rate)` when `settlement_date.year >= 2019`
3. Result: resi margins go negative 2021-2023 (wholesale > capped revenue), recovering 2024+

**Expected outcome**:
- resi/elec: drops from 10.2% to realistic range (negative 2021-2022, 2-5% 2024)
- resi/gas: drops from 11.1% toward -5% to -6% range
- Net margin overall drops (fewer profitable resi years)
- Realistic company behaviour: would have made losses on resi customers during crisis

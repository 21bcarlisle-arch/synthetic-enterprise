## PART 1 — The site is neater but has lost its way

**The director, verbatim:** *"The website is neater but has lost its way a lot. I don't get any of it anymore. I can't follow what the world is doing, what the supplier is doing, and what this is like for an individual customer and their bill, payments, products etc. And ultimately their CO2."*

**Advisor's admission of cause (own it in the retro):** the site-reorientation steer instructed that the narrative spine should follow the pitch's argument. That was a half-truth, and it produced a well-made **pitch document** where an **operational window** used to be. The director's standing brief has three jobs, not one: (1) his live window on the system, (2) the sales and promotion tool, (3) the results-of-actions control surface he steers from. The v4 reorientation served (2) and gutted (1) and (3).

**The requirement:** the site must let the director follow, at any moment, four things — and follow them *as running state, not as argument*:

1. **What the world is doing** — the weather, the market, prices, the regime it is in right now and how it got there.
2. **What the supplier is doing** — hedging, pricing, billing, collections, cash, what decisions it took and why.
3. **What this is like for an individual customer** — a real named-or-numbered household: their consumption, their bill, their payments, their products, their arrears if any, their experience.
4. **And ultimately their CO₂** — the per-customer carbon trajectory, which is now the mission's unit of account.

The pitch narrative and the operational window must **coexist**, not compete: the outsider reads the argument, the director reads the state, and honest state *is* the argument (the pitch itself stakes that claim). How you structure that — layered doors, a separate operator view, drill-downs from narrative into live state — is your design. **What is not acceptable is a site the director cannot follow.**

Note the natural convergence: this is close to the director's own long-standing four-section framing (world / supplier / customers / project). That prior structure exists in canon; reconcile with it rather than inventing a third.

## PART 2 — The segmentation is too thin for the mission

**The director, verbatim:** *"I also think we really need to work on new segmentation to get our growing list of cohorts with combinations or clusters of needs, attitudes and behaviours."*

The ratified v4 purpose rests on personalisation: value lives in the **permutations** of orthogonal factors, not in coarse cohorts. Today's population model does not carry that structure at the depth the mission now requires — and part 1 is blocked by it, because **you cannot show a cohort the model does not have**.

**Requirements:**
- Cohorts defined by **combinations and clusters** across the factor axes — need (house physics and occupancy), attitudes and values, engagement capacity and behaviour — not by any single axis and not by demographics alone.
- **Derived from data, not asserted.** Cluster or estimate joint structure from the linked evidence base; where a combination is asserted rather than estimated, declare it as a simplification with provenance.
- **Independence discipline holds:** generation anchors and validation anchors from disjoint sources; the company never validates discovered cohorts against SIM ground truth.
- **Score on the worst cell, not the average** — the whole point of the permutation space is that the valuable and dangerous cases are rare combinations, not the population centre.
- Atom A (scope-of-need scoring frame) is the scoring frame for this; this steer is about the **population model itself** having the structure the frame is meant to score.

**Where it sits:** this is the trait axis of the hyperpersonalisation thesis (D-SEGMENT). The state layer (timing, events, salience) is a separate, later track — do not conflate them. Traits first: they set the distribution the states move within.

# DIRECTOR STEER — Reframe the segmentation objective: LEARNING value, data-guided, not P&L weights (2026-07-20)

**Type:** [STEER] — a correction to the stage-4 value-frontier objective, before the two values-calls are confirmed and before the generator is wired. The director has caught a genuine conflation; do not proceed on the old framing. Method yours.

## The conflation the director caught

The stage-4 frontier optimises toward **value of the customer** (P&L contribution). The director's point:

> **"Value makes no sense. Bad debt is negative value but working out how to deal with them is really valuable."**

Two different things were collapsed into one:
1. **Value of the customer** — their margin contribution. A fuel-poor household in arrears is negative here.
2. **Value of MODELLING that segment well** — how much the business gains from understanding it. Arrears, vulnerability and collections are *high* on this axis precisely because they are hard, costly, and where regulatory and mortality risk concentrate.

The design has been maximising (1) when the director wants (2). Concretely, **bad-debt-risk currently sits in the weighting as a value REDUCTION — that is backwards.** High bad-debt risk should *raise* a segment's modelling priority, because handling it well is where money and compliance are won or lost. This is the inverted-bell-curve thesis (value concentrates at the difficult edges) colliding with a metric that rewards the comfortable middle.

## The reframed objective

Optimise for **information / learning value: which segments teach the company the most** — not which customers are profitable. A segment ranks high when it is:
- **behaviourally distinct** (acts differently in ways that change a decision), OR
- **commercially consequential in EITHER direction** (large positive *or* large negative P&L impact — arrears and default count as high-consequence, not low-value), OR
- **regulatorily or vulnerability-critical** (protection, priority services, fuel poverty, prepayment, debt).

Under this framing the "protected critical groups" are not a floor bolted onto a profit ranking — they are simply high-learning-value segments, for the same reason everything else on the list is: hard, consequential, and where getting it wrong is expensive. That is cleaner; prefer it.

## The deeper instruction — let the DATA guide, do not guess weights

The director, verbatim: *"this is going to be iterative and we need to let the data guide us rather than just guess."*

So the fixed-weight approach (margin 0.40 / retention 0.25 / …) is itself the thing to move away from — hand-assigned weights are a guess dressed as precision. Requirements:
- **Derive segment importance from the data**, not from a priori weights. Candidate approaches to evaluate, not adopt blindly: measure how much each segment's behaviour actually *varies* (variance it explains in outcomes), how much its presence changes a decision (decision-sensitivity / value-of-information), and how far its behaviour is *not* predictable from the others (distinctness). Where a weight or threshold is unavoidable, it is **chosen, declared, and sensitivity-tested** — same discipline as the 1.5× threshold.
- **Iterate.** This is explicitly a learning loop: propose the reframed metric, show the segment ranking and the re-derived knee it produces, and expect the director to react and refine. Do not treat any single output as final.
- **Re-derive the ~12-segment knee** under the corrected objective. It may move — that is information, not error. Report how and why it changed from the P&L-weighted version.

## What this changes about the two values-calls

The two [ACT]s change shape:
- The "business-value weighting" is **withdrawn as a fixed weight** — replaced by the data-derived learning-value metric above. If any director-set parameter genuinely remains, surface only that, marked *asserted* and sensitivity-tested.
- The "critical/protected groups" list **stays and should if anything widen**: regulatory protection matters a great deal to the director. State plainly that no metric — learning-value included — can ever demote a regulatorily-significant group (vulnerability, fuel poverty, prepayment, priority-services, debt) below full-fidelity modelling. Protection is a floor outside the ranking, not a term inside it.

## The generator wiring stays reserved

Unchanged: the generator change is the director's gate. This steer reframes the *analysis*; it does not authorise wiring. Propose the reframed frontier and the re-derived knee first; the director will react before anything touches the population generator.

**Risk & proportionality:** analysis only, doc-and-data, reversible — and it prevents baking a wrong objective into the population, which would have been expensive to unwind. Tag: **narrow/reversible; the generator change remains a wall.** Report in prose + committed structure per the loop.

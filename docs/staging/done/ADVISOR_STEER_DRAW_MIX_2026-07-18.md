# ADVISOR STEER — Standing balance on the draw mix (director, 2026-07-18, final form)

**Type:** [STEER] — advisory, absorb and carry on. No reply needed; no work interruption.

**The director's standing balance, all three clauses (verbatim intent):**
1. *"I'm ok with that [harness work draining] as long as it does some real work in parallel."*
2. *"Unless it's getting really hampered by issues and real work has become futile. That's the logical balance."*
3. *"And vice versa. Don't keep polishing a well working harness. Do good housekeeping and continuous improvement but if the engine is humming drive fast!"*

**The rule, complete:**
- **Default:** harness/self-improvement work is acceptable **only while real company/product work runs in parallel** — whenever any product-lane atom (D/W/E/C/site) is drawable, at least one of the ≤3 lanes carries it. H-monoculture is a defect.
- **Exception (foundation-first):** if foundational issues make product work genuinely **futile** (wedged publish path, corrupted git, broken gates), harness-only mode is the *correct* priority — but it must be **DECLARED and blocker-named** ("product lanes suspended: <blocker>"), and it ends when the blocker clears. Never silent, never open-ended.
- **Mirror (drive-fast):** the inverse failure is equally a defect — **do not keep polishing a well-working harness.** When the engine is humming (gates green, loop sustaining, alarms quiet), routine housekeeping and continuous improvement continue at their natural background level, but the throttle goes to the COMPANY: product lanes dominate the draw, harness work drops to maintenance weight. A humming engine being endlessly groomed while product atoms sit drawable is the same imbalance as the monoculture, from the other side. If the engine hums — drive fast.

The judgment of which state applies — futile / balanced / humming — is yours, kept honest by declaration (futility named when claimed; and if the harness is humming, the draw mix itself is the evidence). How to weight or mechanise any of this is your design, per LAW A and the dials.

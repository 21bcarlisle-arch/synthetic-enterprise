CONTEXT (advisor deep research, 2026-07-04): a real UK supplier assembles ~20+ SaaS categories: retail core (Kraken/ENSEK/Gentrack), ETRM (Brady/EnergyOne/ION), market messaging (ElectraLink DTS/DIP), data platform (Snowflake/dbt/BI), finance-ERP (NetSuite/SAP), engagement (Braze/CDPs), debt ecosystem (collections platforms, DCA panels, debt sale, credit bureaux), process mining (Celonis), energy margin analytics (Gorilla -- whose new "Energy Margin Intelligence" category is literally Poesys's consistency-gated margin-in-every-decision capability, venture-funded as a standalone product), CX/contact AI, iPaaS, ITSM/observability, KYC, ESG.

THE TAXONOMY -- every category, and every Poesys module, classifies into one of three buckets:
A -- ELIMINATED BY ARCHITECTURE: software that exists only to compensate for fragmentation (CDP, iPaaS, process mining, margin intelligence, most BI). An event-native single-data-model company does not need them. Poesys does not recreate these; it makes them unnecessary.
B -- RECREATED, INTEGRATED: native functions Poesys builds (billing, CRM, ETRM/hedging/risk, finance core, collections, engagement decisioning, regulatory stack). Uniquely spans retail AND trading, which no vendor does.
C -- INTERFACED AT THE BOUNDARY: real-world rails (credit bureaux, payment/PSP, DCC comms, message delivery, KYC). Neither recreated nor eliminated -- these become epistemic-boundary adapters at go-live, same pattern as the market-feed swappable adapter (PV).

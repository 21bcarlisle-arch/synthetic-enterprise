CONTEXT: QJ's evidence surfaces honestly show churn recall at 0.0% (PASSIVE_CHURN_CAP structural limit) and the flagship divergence case is a false POSITIVE (C_IC1 2018: company said 95%, truth 5% -- a real supplier would have torched retention budget on a customer who was never leaving). PART 1 -- MOMENTS OF TRUTH (act on causes and windows, not scores):
Commercial interventions trigger on observable moments, robust to weak prediction:
- Renewal window opening (churn concentrates here -- intervene here regardless of model score)
- Price-competitiveness drift (company tariff vs observable market -- a cause the company controls)
- Service-recovery events (complaint, billing error, arrears entry)
- Payment-stress signals (timing slip, missed payment -- already modelled)
Each trigger enters a DECISION, not an automatic action.

PART 2 -- EV-BASED DECISIONS (the three-horizon model made operational):
Every decision is an expected-value calculation logged AT DECISION TIME: action options (offer X, payment plan, do nothing, managed exit), each with cost, CLV-at-risk, estimated probability the action changes the outcome. Chosen action + its H1 expected value recorded. Note: 14/14 retention acceptance is itself a red flag -- offers never declined are over-generous or aimed at customers who would have stayed. The EV frame surfaces this automatically.

PART 3 -- H1 vs H2 VARIANCE: every decision's expected value tracked against realized outcome; the variance stream feeds back into offer calibration. The company learns to ACT well, not just estimate well.

PART 4 -- COUNTERFACTUAL LIFT (the SIM superpower, extend Phase NO): for every intervention class, run the matched no-intervention counterfactual and measure TRUE lift -- the thing no real company can measure. Lift-per-pound becomes the fitness function for retention policy; Digital Darwinism operates on POLICIES, not just models.

## The design principle this establishes (record it as canon when framed)

**Model the fundamentals; generate the residual as a calibrated stochastic process with honest tails and regime behaviour; never pretend to forecast the unforecastable.** Concretely:
- Fundamentals (modellable): weather→demand coupling, merit-order stack, storage/flow seasonality, capacity evolution. These the SIM should *cause*.
- Residual (irreducible at horizon): outage timing, geopolitics, sentiment spikes. These the SIM should *draw* from calibrated distributions with the right tail mass and regime structure — the spike-tail defect is exactly this residual being under-drawn.
- **Mean reversion holds within a regime and breaks across regimes** (2021–22 is the proof case). A single-regime mean-reverting model is disqualified by our own decade record.
- **The wall split for beliefs:** recency, sentiment, adaptive expectations are properties of the COMPANY's belief formation, not of the true process — model them company-side and score them (the 120-day trailing estimator is already an instance: a recency-biased belief the desk must outgrow).

CLAUSE 1 -- MORTAL. Two terminal states end a run: INSOLVENCY (cash below zero, credit exhausted -- treasury/FRA machinery already detects it) and LICENCE REVOCATION (sustained compliance breach -- scorecard becomes binding). Endgame: the LONGEVITY TOURNAMENT -- repeated lifetime runs, scored on survival duration x cumulative value; strategies compete on the distribution. EV becomes survival-weighted. The 2021-22 crisis that killed 28 real suppliers can now kill ours; a dead run is data.

CLAUSE 2 -- BRAND AS BEHAVIOURAL PHYSICS (SIM attributes the benefit; EV emerges). Four mechanisms, per customer, segment-conditional: (a) RELATIVE PRICE ELASTICITY -- the savings required to move a customer is a function of relative brand strength (conjoint framing: unknown/poorly-perceived brands must offer MORE); this drives both retention stickiness and acquisition cost. (b) FORGIVENESS BUFFER -- strong brand absorbs a service failure without triggering in-market entry; weak brand does not. (c) SENSITISED EXIT -- failure costs twice: some leave instantly, the rest carry a PRIMED lower switching threshold to the next normal opportunity (damage has memory). (d) NEEDS-MATCH -- fit is more than service: tariff terms, green credentials for values-driven segments, simplicity for others (dovetails the customer-preference model). VALUATION: no separate brand asset line (double-counting); reputation works only through causal channels, and enters EV as a TERMINAL-VALUE MULTIPLE (a 4.6-star book commands a premium multiple over identical cashflows at 2.1 stars) -- the multiple is the emergent summary of (a)-(d). The company never sees the parameters; it infers them from its own win/loss data (conjoint-style) -- hypothesis-test-evidence with a commercial prize. ANCHORING: Ofgem consumer research/switching trials, published energy conjoint and WTP studies, brand-premium literature; reasoned flagged inference where gaps.

CLAUSE 3 -- REGULATED EXPERIMENTATION. The hypothesis engine operates within regulatory and legal parameters: consumer-duty limits on differential pricing, fairness constraints policed by the company's own compliance layer, reputational blowback if detected. Learning comes from natural experiments, observational inference, and the limited tests it can legitimately run. Slower learning is the honest metric; compliance-vs-learning tension is a feature (legal pushes back on commercial).

# DIRECTOR CAMPAIGN FRAMING — Epoch 2: the coupled world, ranked by value (2026-07-19)

**Type:** director's campaign direction, transmitted by the advisor. Requirements and non-negotiables only — the mechanisms, sequencing detail, and statistical methods are yours to design and discover.

## What the director wants covered

His words, as the scope of this campaign: *"sorting weather, linked to energy prices, linked to house shape, proper billing with real event delays, and then choosing decent customer segments to cover the scope of need not just frequency."*

## The five governing requirements

**1. Segments define the SCORING FRAME, and they are chosen for scope of NEED, not frequency.**
Before the physics deepens, define the grid you will be scored against: customer archetypes × market regimes, chosen to span the *range of need* — the cases that stress different physics (affordability and collections; unusual consumption shape; export and self-generation; pass-through commercial; crisis vs calm regimes) — not the cases that are numerous. The fidelity score is the **worst-explained cell of that grid, not the population average.** A model that nails the comfortable middle and explains nothing at the edges has failed, however good its average fit. Deepen segment realism later; define the frame now, because it determines what "good" means for everything built after it.

**2. VALUE is the arbiter of which gaps to close — not gap size.**
Measured belief-vs-truth gaps (as now exist for the payment triad) rank fidelity work only when weighted by **exposure**: what is at risk if the company is this wrong, here. The company may legitimately choose to be more wrong in one area than another — being materially wrong where wrongness is cheap is a correct allocation, not a defect. Director's framing: *"it may choose to be wrong in one area more than another and only value can help it decide."* Note the deliberate circularity: this is why the value cycle is Epoch 2 itself, not something that comes after — the company needs enough of a value model to rank its own fidelity investments.

**3. The market and cost logic must be DISCOVERED, not handed over.**
The advisor is explicitly NOT supplying a causal chain, because a sketched chain becomes a specification and the advisor's simplifications get built as physics. Do the discovery properly, from real sources, and frame it yourself. The director's own correction of an advisor over-simplification, recorded here as an indication of the depth required rather than as a spec:

> *"National wholesale price is an output at a national level of global energy prices and weather. Itself driven by industrial and residential demand and interconnection. This drives the supplier to price for retail products bought now. But otherwise, if fixed prices bought before, the actual price doesn't change."*

So: how GB wholesale price actually forms, and — separately and distinctly — how a **supplier's actual cost** behaves (a weighted average of prior purchases, not the spot print), and therefore *where* a price move actually bites a supplier and where it does not. Get this right; it is load-bearing for everything downstream. One advisor **hypothesis, offered to be tested or refuted by your discovery, not to be built**: that for a hedged supplier the dominant weather coupling runs through *volume* (consumption deviating from hedged quantity, with the increment transacted at the moment it is most expensive) rather than through the price of already-purchased energy. Treat as a claim to check against evidence.

**4. Correlations and dependence structures must be ESTIMATED from data — mathematics and statistics, not hand-set constants.**
Director: *"it can use logic and data to determine the nature and strength of these correlations, inter-relationships and so on. Ideally maths and stats driven."* The nature AND the strength of every relationship in the cascade should be derived from real observed data with defensible statistical method, and the **joint tail behaviour matters more than the marginal behaviour** — the dangerous states are the ones where several conditions coincide, and a model calibrated on averages will erase exactly the coincidences that kill suppliers. Where a relationship is asserted rather than estimated, say so honestly and register it as a simplification.

**5. Randomness lives behind the curtain.**
The stochastic structure — the generating processes, their parameters, their dependence — belongs inside the SIM and is hidden. The company sees only observables and must infer structure from them, imperfectly, as a real supplier does. The epistemic wall is not relaxed for this campaign; the measured belief-vs-truth gap *is* the point.

## Ordering

The scoring grid (requirement 1) is defined first because it defines success. Exact sequencing, stage-by-stage, is yours to propose within this order. **W1 BUILD remains closed — this campaign proceeds through DISCOVER and FRAME until the director opens it.**

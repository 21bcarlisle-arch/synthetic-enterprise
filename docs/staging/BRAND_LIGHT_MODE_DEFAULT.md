# BRAND: light mode is the default — black on white (P1, director-decided)

**Staged:** 2026-07-13 by advisor, **director-decided**. Disposition: QUEUE
(rank with BRAND1/SITE work; not an interrupt).

**Director, verbatim:** *"My only niggle is the black background in the new
webpages. I prefer black on white to white on black. Same for docs etc. Only
white on black for impact, used sparingly."*

## The decision (amend the BRAND CONSTITUTION)
- **DEFAULT: black on white.** Light background, dark text. This is the ground
  state for the site, for documents, and for any generated artefact.
- **Dark (white-on-black) is an ACCENT, used SPARINGLY, for IMPACT only.**
  Legitimate uses: a hero moment, a single statement panel, a deliberate
  full-bleed break between sections, a headline figure. It is punctuation, not
  paper.
- **The rule of thumb:** if dark is used on most of a page, it has stopped being
  impact and become a theme — which is precisely what the director does not
  want. If in doubt, light.

## Rationale (record it, so it survives)
This is consistent with the brand's own stated philosophy — **architectural,
restrained, colour as information not decoration.** Dark backgrounds do the
opposite: they make everything look emphatic, so nothing is. Black-on-white is
the neutral ground against which the BRAG palette (blue=verified, red=blocked,
amber=at risk, green=on track) actually reads as INFORMATION. On a black field,
those colours glow and shout; on white, they signal. **The restraint is the
point.**
Also practical: this is a document-heavy, evidence-heavy site — tables, numbers,
ledgers, passports, long-form method. Light backgrounds are what long-form
reading and printed/exported documents want.

## Scope (apply everywhere, not just the new doors)
- Site: all doors, existing and forthcoming (3-8), plus the front door.
- Design tokens: flip the default surface/ink tokens; dark becomes a named
  ACCENT surface, not the base.
- Documents, exports, PDFs, the board pack, any generated artefact.
- The brand rules file the agents load — so new work is born light, not
  retrofitted.

## Non-negotiables
- **Accessibility holds:** maintain contrast ratios on the light ground; the
  BRAG colours must remain distinguishable and WCAG-compliant against white
  (they were tuned against black — re-check them, do not assume they carry over).
- Do not simply invert. Colours tuned for a dark field often go muddy or
  garish on white. Re-tune the palette for the light ground and re-run the brand
  compliance check.
- Preserve the exemplar: keep the dark treatment in the brand doc as the
  documented ACCENT case, with an example of correct sparing use.

## DoD
Brand constitution and design tokens amended (light default, dark as accent);
all site doors converted and pixel-verified; BRAG palette re-tuned and
contrast-checked against white; agent-loadable brand rules updated so new work is
born correct; brand compliance test updated to FAIL a page that uses dark as its
base. One digest line.

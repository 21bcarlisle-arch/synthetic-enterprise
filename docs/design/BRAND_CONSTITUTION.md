# BRAND_CONSTITUTION.md — Poesys brand identity & AI-usable design schema

**Status:** Director-decided 2026-07-13 (live conversation with advisor; direction, palette,
wordmark, glyph grammar and exemplar all explicitly ratified by Rich — "love it").
**Tier:** 3 (novel, reversible — flag-and-proceed).
**Lane:** L2 SITE per THREE_LANES — `site/**` plus document templates; disjoint from BUILD,
runs parallel, never gates an epoch.
**Place in the arc:** The brand is durable IP alongside the harness and method (pitch/demo
asset for S4 method casebook and S5 go-live; every future customer-facing surface inherits
it). Independent of Epoch-2 BUILD work. Sits ABOVE `SITE_CONSTITUTION.md` /
`POESYS_SITE_BRIEF.md` (in_progress): those govern the site's structure and behaviour; this
governs the visual and verbal identity of EVERY Poesys surface — site, charts, bill PDFs,
reports, board packs, pitch decks, digests, NTFYs. Where the site constitution and this
document conflict on visual identity, this document wins; reconcile rather than fork.

---

## 1. The problem

Poesys has no brand system. Every surface currently improvises its own colours, type and
status conventions. Because every Poesys artefact is generated by AI, a conventional brand
book (a PDF for humans to interpret) is useless here: the guidelines' consumer is the agent.
The requirement is a brand identity **in a form an AI loads and obeys** — machine-readable
tokens, short testable rules, and canonical exemplars — such that any agent generating any
surface produces on-brand output by construction, and drift is detectable by test.

## 2. Identity (decided)

- Inspiration: **first direct** — the anti-incumbent stance. Radically plain, human,
  honest. Poesys is **the energy supplier that hides nothing**.
- Visual character: architectural simplicity. Black and white carry ALL structure;
  typography, weight and space do all hierarchy work. No ornament, no drawn imagery as
  identity, no gradients, no decoration. Elegance of simple design, never tech for tech's
  sake.
- The company's existing honesty conventions — clock/basis labels, provisional badges,
  published misses, reconciliation bridges — ARE the brand voice. Plain English, precise,
  no superlatives, every figure carries its basis. Formalise what already exists; invent
  nothing.

## 3. Palette (decided) — BRAG

Black, white, and four status colours — blue, red, amber, green — each in a **bright** and
a **soft** version. Twelve colour values plus black and white, total. Reference values from
the ratified exemplar (§7); these are the starting canon — refinements for
contrast/accessibility may be proposed but any palette change is **director-ratified only**
(brand dials face the director, same law as R13 curriculum dials):

| Role  | Bright    | Soft      | Bright vs white (WCAG) |
|-------|-----------|-----------|------------------------|
| Blue  | `#0047FF` | `#DCE7FF` | 6.28:1 (AA)            |
| Red   | `#E11900` | `#FFDDD6` | 4.84:1 (AA)            |
| Amber | `#AA6700` | `#FFF0C9` | 4.52:1 (AA)            |
| Green | `#00873C` | `#D6F2E0` | 4.63:1 (AA)            |

Black `#0A0A0A`, white `#FFFFFF`; greys are tints of black for hairlines/secondary text only.

**Amber re-tune (director-ratified 2026-07-13, BRAND_LIGHT_MODE_DEFAULT.md):** with the LIGHT
ground now the default (§3a), the BRAG brights were contrast-checked against white. Three
cleared WCAG AA unchanged. Amber's prior `#F5A600` read only **2.03:1** on white (fails even
the 3:1 large-text/UI floor — it glowed on black but went garish/illegible on white), so it
was darkened to the warmest ochre-amber that clears AA, `#AA6700` (4.52:1). Softs are pale
fills behind black text and are unaffected. This is the one palette value the light-default
switch changed.

**BRAG status semantics (decided, universal):**
blue = **verified done** (earned — expert-hour-passed / at target, never self-declared);
green = **on track**; amber = **at risk / provisional / estimated**; red = **blocked / failing**.

## 3a. Ground: light is the default, dark is a sparing accent (director-decided 2026-07-13)

**BRAND_LIGHT_MODE_DEFAULT.md, director verbatim:** *"My only niggle is the black background
in the new webpages. I prefer black on white to white on black. Same for docs etc. Only white
on black for impact, used sparingly."*

- **DEFAULT: black on white.** Light background (`surface.base` = white, `surface.sunken` =
  grey-05), dark ink (`ink.base` = structural black). This is the ground state for the site,
  for documents, and for any generated artefact. New work is **born light**.
- **Dark (white-on-black) is an ACCENT, used SPARINGLY, for IMPACT only** — the named
  `surface.accent-dark` role (structural black, `ink.on-accent` white). Legitimate uses: a
  hero moment, a single statement panel, a deliberate full-bleed break between sections, a
  headline figure. It is **punctuation, not paper**.
- **Rule of thumb / compliance line:** if dark is the BASE of a page (the body/root surface),
  it has stopped being impact and become a theme — precisely what the director does not want.
  A page whose base surface is dark **fails** brand compliance
  (`tools/brand_compliance.base_surface_is_dark`, mutation-tested). If in doubt, light.
- **Rationale (recorded so it survives):** the brand's own philosophy is architectural,
  restrained — colour as information, not decoration. On a black field the BRAG palette glows
  and shouts, so nothing signals; on white it reads as information. The restraint is the point.
  Practically, this is a document-heavy, evidence-heavy identity (tables, ledgers, passports,
  long-form method) — light is what long-form reading and printed/exported documents want.
- **Preserved exemplar of correct sparing dark use:** the §7 exemplar's terminal status block
  (`.term`, black ground / white ink demonstrating the glyph grammar surviving without colour)
  and `site/brand/proof.html`'s matching block are the documented ACCENT case — one small dark
  panel on an otherwise white page. That is the ceiling for dark: a single panel, not a theme.

## 4. The laws (decided, non-negotiable)

1. **Colour is information, never decoration.** If it is coloured, it is a status or a
   defined data category. Everything else is black, white, grey.
2. **Bright = live/current state. Soft = historical, backgrounds, fills, projections.**
3. **Soft colours never appear as text or thin lines** — fills behind black text only
   (accessibility: pastel-on-white fails contrast).
4. **One typeface.** A single neo-grotesque sans family (exemplar uses the Helvetica
   Neue/Arial stack; a self-hosted equivalent may be proposed). Hierarchy through weight,
   size and space — never through a second face or ornament. No monospace second face; the
   glyph grammar carries the technical flavour.
5. **Glyphs are information, never decoration** (§6). Six glyphs is the ceiling; each has
   exactly one meaning, everywhere, forever. A tilde that ever means anything other than
   "estimated" destroys the grammar.
6. **Type-only identity.** Wordmark: lowercase **`poesys.`** — with the full stop — in the
   house face, black (white on black). No symbol, no drawn logo. Tiny spaces
   (favicon/app icon/avatar): solid black square, white lowercase `p`, same face.

## 5. Deliverables (required properties; implementation is the agent's)

1. **A single machine-readable token source** — W3C design-tokens-format JSON: the twelve
   colours + black/white/greys, type scale, spacing scale, weights. The ONLY place a
   colour value ever lives. Every surface (site CSS, Chart.js palettes, bill PDF
   generation, report/deck templates) consumes tokens, never raw values.
2. **A brand rules file the agent loads** — CLAUDE.md-style: short, imperative, testable
   statements (§4 + §6 essentially verbatim), referenced before generating any visual
   surface. Not prose guidance — rules.
3. **Canonical exemplars** — the ratified mock in §7 (front-door page, customer bill,
   glyph grammar) preserved as reference implementations that generation copies from
   rather than reinterprets. Exemplars beat prose for preventing drift.
4. **Enforcement** — brand compliance must be testable in the existing harness pattern:
   at minimum, (a) no raw colour value exists on any surface outside the token source;
   (b) soft-colour-as-text is detectable/blocked. Mechanism is the agent's choice.

## 6. Glyph grammar (decided)

Native characters of the house face — no image assets, work in any medium including plain
text (NTFYs, digests, markdown, commit messages carry status without colour):

| Glyph | Name      | Meaning (the only meaning)                             | Colour twin |
|-------|-----------|--------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
| `.`   | full stop | Done. Verified, final, closed. The wordmark's own mark.| blue        |
| `_`   | cursor    | In build. Live now, being written, on track.           | green       |
| `~`   | tilde     | Estimated, provisional, approximate.                   | amber       |
| `!`   | bang      | Blocked, failing, needs attention.                     | red         |
| `>`   | prompt    | Action, go, next. Links, CTAs, instructions.           | —           |
| `//`  | comment   | The working: basis, provenance, clock labels.          | —           |

Canonical plain-text status line: `billing . | trading _ | gas read ~ | settlement !`

Functional applications already ratified in the exemplar: provisional headline figures
carry a leading tilde (`~£1,505,250`); every basis/clock annotation is a `//` comment;
estimated meter reads are `~ ESTIMATED` with `~` on the consumption figure; CTAs end `>`.

hero, BRAG company-health rows including an honest red, headline figures with `~` and `//`
basis lines; (2) customer bill — actual vs `~ ESTIMATED` reads, soft-fill status chips,

## 8. Definition of done

1. Token source exists, is the sole home of every colour/type/spacing value, and at least
   the live site consumes it end-to-end (R11: verified on the rendered pixel, not the
   commit).
2. Brand rules file exists and is loaded by whatever generates visual surfaces.
3. Exemplar preserved verbatim as the canonical reference; site front door and one real
   customer bill surface demonstrably follow it (BRAG chips, `~`/`//`/`>` in live use).
4. Brand-compliance check runs in the harness (no raw colour outside tokens; no
   soft-as-text) and passes.
5. Rollout of remaining surfaces (charts, PDFs, reports, decks, NTFY/digest glyph
   conventions) may be registered as follow-up atoms rather than done in one pass —
   batch honestly, don't big-bang.

## 10. Live-site adoption (L2→L3, 2026-07-13/14)

The token source (§5.1) is only real when the LIVE site consumes it. The adoption mechanism:

- **`site/brand/brand.css`** — the single stylesheet a production page links. It `@import`s
  `tokens.css` (the byte-pinned projection of `tokens.json`, the sole colour home) and maps the
  site's long-standing semantic variables (`--bg`, `--surface`, `--text`, `--green`, `--red`,
  `--amber`, `--blue`, and the legacy `--teal`/`--purple`) onto BRAG tokens. It contains **no
  raw hex** (`test_shared_brand_css_has_no_raw_hex`). The off-brand accents collapse onto the
  four BRAG status colours — colour is information, never decoration (law 1). It also carries the
  reusable brand components (`.wordmark`, `.brag--*` status chips, glyph helpers) so on-brand
  output is produced by construction.

- **Adopted pages** link `brand.css`, delete their own `:root` palette, and render the `poesys.`
  wordmark in place of the legacy `⚡ Poesys` symbol (law 6, type-only identity). The enforced
  **adoption frontier** (`tests/tools/test_brand_compliance.py::_ADOPTED_LIVE_SURFACES`) is:
  `site/index.html` (front door), `method/`, `platform/`, `project/`, `sim/`, `simplified/`.
  Each is proven: no raw hex in its `<style>`, a light base surface resolved **through the linked
  cascade** (the harness follows `<link>` + `@import`), and the semantic colours resolving to the
  ratified BRAG token hexes end-to-end (`test_adopted_pages_resolve_brag_status_colours_end_to_end`).

- **The frontier grows.** A page joins only once its CSS surface is genuinely token-only.
  Chart.js palettes (`<script>` colour strings), bill PDFs, reports/decks, and the remaining
  pages — including the generator-templated `company/` and `method-casebook/` — are declared
  follow-up atoms (batch honestly, no big-bang), consistent with §8.5.

- **R11 residual:** the live-pixel-with-the-director's-eyes check is his to make. Autonomously,
  the harness resolves the live CSS cascade to the token values (the strongest check without a
  browser); the rendered-pixel confirmation on the deployed site remains the director's.

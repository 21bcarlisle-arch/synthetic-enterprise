# UK Feed-in Tariff (FiT) Levy — £/MWh by Year (2016–2024)

Research for Phase 31a. Compiled 2026-06-22.

## Summary

The Feed-in Tariff (FiT) levelisation levy is collected from all licensed UK electricity
suppliers proportional to their share of total GB electricity supply. No domestic exemption
exists — residential, SME, and I&C customers all pay the same £/MWh rate. This distinguishes
FiT from CCL (domestic-exempt) and makes it structurally similar to CM (universal).

**Rate range:** £4.10–£8.47/MWh across 2016–2024.  
**Key pattern:** Rising 2016→2020 as fleet matured; dip in 2021/22; rising again 2022–2025.

## Key Pattern Analysis

**Why does 2021 dip below 2020?**
The scheme was closed to new applicants from October 2019 (large sites) and January 2020
(all). Newer installations that entered the scheme in the final years before closure
received lower generation tariffs (the scheme used degression — tariffs reduced quarterly
as deployment volumes hit triggers). These lower-tariff installs reduced average scheme
cost even as the number of sites increased. Scheme cost fell from £1.76bn (SY11) to
£1.57bn (SY12) despite no reduction in installed capacity.

**Why does 2024 appear higher than 2023?**
The 2024 figure is the **initial** billing rate (0.847 p/kWh), not a reconciled rate.
The initial rate is set quarterly and later adjusted. The reconciliation for 2024/25 is
due November 2026. It may be revised down to ~£7.50–8.00/MWh. The npower table shows
initial rates systematically above reconciled rates.

**RPI indexation vs generation decline:**
From 2022 onwards, older schemes (pre-2016 installs) receive RPI-indexed tariffs that
outpace the modest decline in generation as some smaller sites exit. This keeps costs
elevated even as total installed capacity stops growing.

## Implementation Notes

- **Obligation year:** Apr-Mar, same as RO/CM. Use `_ro_oy_start_year()` helper.
- **No domestic exemption:** unlike CCL, resi customers pay FiT levy.
- **All segments:** resi, SME, I&C all pay the same £/MWh rate.
- **Clamp behaviour:** pre-2016 → 2016 rate; post-2024 → 2024 rate (conservative).
- **2024 caveat:** initial billing rate; reconciliation pending. May be ~10% lower.

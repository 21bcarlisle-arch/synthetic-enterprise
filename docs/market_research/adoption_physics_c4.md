# C4_adoption_physics — DISCOVER findings: anchoring ToU/DER adoption susceptibility

**Atom:** `C4_adoption_physics` (epoch 4, `docs/design/maturity_map.yaml`). **Status:** DISCOVER
only, per ADOPTION_JOURNEY_REGISTER.md's own "registered, do not start" instruction and epoch
gating. This DISCOVER pass answers the atom's own registered open question: *"anchor sources for
adoption/threshold distributions."*

## Overall incidence — real, current (2026), genuinely low

- **[L] UK ToU tariff adoption is low: estimates range ~2.8% to 9% of homes** (source-dependent —
  one figure covers the latest full tariff-reading year, July 2023-June 2024; another gives a
  narrower "smart ToU tariffs specifically" figure). Either way, this confirms ToU/DER adoption is a
  genuine minority behaviour today, not a plausible default — directly supporting the atom's own
  framing that adoption is a JOURNEY with real friction, not a switch most customers simply flip.

## Segment-varying shape — real, decisive, not flat

- **[L] Nesta's own GB consumption-pattern research finds specific "tech-affluent" household
  profiles show 30-40% ToU adoption** — an order of magnitude above the population average. This is
  a real, sourced, DECISIVE anchor that adoption susceptibility should NOT be modelled as a single
  population-wide rate: a real minority of household archetypes are genuinely much more receptive,
  matching this atom's own "extending the engagement archetypes" language. The susceptibility
  DISTRIBUTION itself (who is more/less receptive) is the real anchor point, not just an aggregate
  incidence number.

## Friction sources — real, concrete, matching the atom's own named traits

- **Tariff complexity / comparison friction:** choosing well requires matching a tariff's time
  structure to one's own hour-by-hour usage pattern, not just comparing a headline unit rate — a
  genuine cognitive/evaluation friction distinct from simple price comparison.
- **Structural flexibility precondition:** benefiting from ToU requires the ABILITY to shift load to
  off-peak periods (appliance timing, EV charging, battery/storage) — this is a real STRUCTURAL
  precondition, not purely an attitudinal one; a customer can be maximally "willing" and still gain
  nothing without genuine load-shifting flexibility (relevant to distinguishing "friction
  sensitivity" from "reward responsiveness" as this atom's own trait list already separates them).
- **Physical onboarding friction:** smart-meter installation lead time is real and current (4-8 weeks
  as of mid-2026) — a genuine time-cost friction point in the adoption journey itself, not just a
  psychological one.

## Regulatory/infrastructure timeline — real, current, relevant to this atom's own epoch placement

- **[L] Ofgem's market-wide half-hourly settlement (MHHS) is rolling out through 2025-2026** — the
  real infrastructure precondition broader ToU tariff availability and adoption depend on. This is
  external confirmation (not just an internal sequencing choice) that this atom's own epoch-4
  placement ("dependencies unbuilt or in flight") matches real-world timing: the underlying
  settlement infrastructure enabling wider ToU adoption is itself still mid-rollout in the real UK
  market as of this project's own "now."

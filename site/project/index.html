About

Poesys is not building a model of an energy supplier; it is building a running autonomous energy business -- one whose commercial, financial, and operational processes all execute, settled continuously against real Elexon/NESO half-hourly settlement data (2016-2025). The company cannot see inside the simulation it runs in: it discovers its world only through what a real supplier could observe -- meter reads, market feeds, customer contacts, bills and payments, regulatory publications. The test on every line of company code is simple: "could a real UK supplier know this?" The final phase of the roadmap is a go-live analysis -- what it would actually take, legally and operationally, to serve a real customer.

It is also a company that can die. Insolvency and licence revocation are terminal states here, not exceptions designed away -- which is what makes the crisis-survival evidence below an earned result, not an assumption: roughly 30 real UK suppliers failed in the 2021-22 crisis, and this one did not.

Three products come out of this, in order: the Method first (the transferable way of directing an autonomous agent to build a company -- the casebook is what makes the rest repeatable), the synthetic data second, and the Platform (the software itself) third. A live, public track record of predictions vs outcomes -- misses included -- runs on the Method page.

Key Discoveries

Regime-Change Blindness -- Revised

An early build showed the hedging model converging toward naked positions in calm markets, mirroring how 28 real UK suppliers failed in 2021-22. A later fix corrected a point-in-time data leak in the volatility calculation, and re-deriving the finding against the current build shows the opposite: hedge cover stays real (0.80-0.90) through every year, calm ones included. The naked-hedging story reflected an earlier, since-superseded model -- caught and corrected, not left stale.

Activity-Based Pricing Gap

Flat margin pricing makes high-cost-to-serve customers net-negative. Large SME customers destroy value under standard pricing.

Price Elasticity Disconfirmed

Rising prices do NOT increase switching propensity. 2022 crisis bills (avg £3,549) produced the lowest switching rate (3-4%). Savings available -- not price level -- drives switching.

Crisis Survival

The supplier survived 2021-22 with treasury drawdown, churn spike, and recovery. 30+ real suppliers failed. The simulation replicates the conditions faithfully.

A UK-wide tax on business energy use (HMRC, not Ofgem), collected by suppliers at the point of delivery. Domestic supply and de minimis business consumption are exempt -- this portfolio is entirely I&C so it is fully in scope.

Ofgem's Guaranteed Standards of Performance. Automatic compensation payable direct to the customer within 10 working days of a missed standard -- a customer-facing penalty, not a policy levy passed through in prices.

Key Regulatory Findings

WHD / ECO Exemption

Both mandatory only for >150k domestic customers. Portfolio has 2–5 residential customers -- EXEMPT for all years. Growth warning: obligation triggers at ~150k customers.

CCL 2019 Spike

April 2019: electricity CCL +45%, gas CCL +67%. Largest single-year policy cost increase in the dataset. I&C pass-through means this hits customers directly.

FiT Scheme Closure

FiT closed to new applicants April 2019. Board section shows cost falling to NIL from 2020. Smart Export Guarantee replaced it but is not a supplier levy.

FRA Capital Post-2022

Ofgem FRA requires combined market + credit stress. Model: equity > (price VaR + credit stress). Board RAG shows FRA compliance per year 2016–2025.

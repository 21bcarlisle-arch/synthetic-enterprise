About

Poesys is a high-fidelity autonomous simulation of a UK electricity and gas supply business, running continuously against real Elexon/NESO half-hourly settlement data (2016-2025). The goal is a simulation detailed enough to say: "that is how a real UK energy supplier works" -- and use it to build and prove the software that would run one autonomously. When the software is proven, the market interfaces swap from simulated to real -- and the company transacts.

Key Discoveries

Regime-Change Blindness

Agent converged to near-naked hedging during 2016-2020 calm, directly before the crisis -- mirroring how 28 real UK suppliers failed in 2021-22. Discovered in simulation before the fix.

Activity-Based Pricing Gap

Flat margin pricing makes high-cost-to-serve customers net-negative. Large SME customers destroy value under standard pricing.

Price Elasticity Disconfirmed

Rising prices do NOT increase switching propensity. 2022 crisis bills (avg £3,549) produced the lowest switching rate (3-4%). Savings available -- not price level -- drives switching.

Crisis Survival

The supplier survived 2021-22 with treasury drawdown, churn spike, and recovery. 30+ real suppliers failed. The simulation replicates the conditions faithfully.

How It Is Built

One human (MD/board) plus two AI roles under a tiered approval model, not a single hand-rolled script. Rich stages direction in docs/staging/ and reviews outcomes; Claude Code designs, delegates and manages the build; qwen3:14b (local Ollama GPU) performs mechanical code generation; a separate local-only risk committee makes simulated business decisions with no frontier API spend.

Every proposal is classified into a tier before it proceeds: Tier 1 one-way doors (architecture, epistemic-wall, safety-control changes) hard-block for explicit in-conversation approval, no timeout; Tier 2 pre-approved queue items (already named in PRIORITIES.md or a staged instruction) start immediately, no opt-out window; Tier 3 novel self-generated proposals keep a 4-hour opt-out window so Rich's veto has real information value.

Permanent Rules (from the verification-week retrospective)
R1. Consumer-verified completion
An artifact with an external consumer is done only when that consumer's fetch confirms it -- quote the fetched evidence.
R2. Long-running processes
A code fix is deployed only once the running process has been restarted with it. Committed does not mean running.
R3. Two-strike redesign
A second false completion claim on the same component means eliminate or redesign the mechanism, not patch it again.
R4. Diagnosis discipline
Before fixing a stuck problem, name the nearest working analogue and state the diff; if none, build the smallest closed-loop test first.
R5. Alerting
Notifications fire on state transitions only, carry the diagnostic payload, never repeat an unchanged status.
R6. Board/report sections
A new dashboard or report section is never the primary work of a phase -- reporting is a byproduct of building capability, not the capability itself.

Full retrospective practice: docs/retrospectives/2026-07-04-verification-week.md.

Core Design Principles
Point-in-Time Blindfold
The company cannot see future data or simulation internals. It discovers the world through meter reads, market feeds, its own bills and regulatory publications.
Real Settlement Data
Prices from Elexon (SSP half-hourly), NESO, and Open-Meteo. Every bill settled against actual historical wholesale prices. The 2021-22 crisis is in the data.
Full Policy Cost Stack
RO, CfD, FiT, Capacity Market, CCL, GGL all modelled per-period. Network charges from Ofgem Annex 9. Domestic price cap from 2019.
Autonomous Operation
Runs on local GPU (qwen3:14b / Ollama). Claude Code orchestrates design. Risk committee runs locally -- no frontier AI spend in sim runs.

Policy Cost Stack

62 regulatory modules wired (Phase OL, 2026-07-03). All levy calculations use real Ofgem/DESNZ published rates. Click any row below for its legal basis.

SchemeKey RateStatus
Renewable Obligation (RO)0.24–0.42 ROCs/MWh; buy-out £44–50/ROC per yearWIRED
Renewables Obligation Order (as amended annually). Buy-out proceeds are recycled to compliant suppliers; the obligation level and buy-out price are published each 1 April. Electricity-only levy -- gas legs are unaffected.
Feed-in Tariff (FiT) Levy£8.36–9.45/MWh elec; closed Apr 2019; NIL from 2020 onwardWIRED
Closed to new applicants 1 April 2019. Existing installations' payments continue to be levelised across all suppliers for their contracted term (typically 20 years) -- NIL from 2020 reflects no *new* obligation being created, not the scheme winding down.
Climate Change Levy (CCL)Elec: 0.583–0.847 p/kWh; Gas: 0.203–0.339 p/kWh; 2019 spike +45%/+67% flaggedWIRED
A UK-wide tax on business energy use (HMRC, not Ofgem), collected by suppliers at the point of delivery. Domestic supply and de minimis business consumption are exempt -- this portfolio is entirely I&C so it is fully in scope.
Guaranteed Standards (GSOP)£30/missed appointment; £30/final bill delay (>6 weeks after account closure)WIRED
Ofgem's Guaranteed Standards of Performance. Automatic compensation payable direct to the customer within 10 working days of a missed standard -- a customer-facing penalty, not a policy levy passed through in prices.
Warm Home Discount (WHD)£140/eligible customer; mandatory only for >150k domestic customers — portfolio EXEMPTEXEMPT
The 150,000-domestic-customer threshold is reviewed annually by Ofgem. Below it, no rebate obligation exists at all -- there is no partial or pro-rated liability to model.
Energy Company Obligation (ECO)ECO1–ECO4 rates; mandatory only for >150k domestic customers — portfolio EXEMPTEXEMPT
Shares the same large-supplier threshold as WHD. Smaller suppliers may participate voluntarily in ECO funding but carry no mandatory installation target -- the growth-trigger point named in the finding below applies to both schemes together.
Carbon EmissionsScope 2 elec: 175–290 gCO2/kWh (real UK grid mix); Scope 1 gas: 183 gCO2/kWhWIRED
Scope 2 electricity intensity follows the National Grid ESO's published annual fuel mix. Scope 1 gas uses a fixed BEIS/DESNZ combustion conversion factor -- gas intensity does not vary year to year the way grid electricity does.
Fuel Mix Disclosure (FMD)Annual disclosure per Electricity (Fuel Mix Disclosure) Regulations; fuel_mix_disclosure.py existsNEXT
Requires suppliers to publish the generation mix behind the electricity they sold, alongside the bill, each year. The calculation module exists; wiring it into the annual board pack is the next Regulatory-tab build item.
Ofgem Supply ReturnCustomer counts by segment, GWh supplied, bad debt/customer; annual filing per year 2016–2025WIRED
An annual regulatory return filed even by suppliers below the large-supplier thresholds -- customer counts, volume supplied and bad debt exposure are reported regardless of portfolio size.
SLC Compliance Scorecard10 SLC domains; per-domain RAG from bad debt / bill clarity / FRA / complaint signalsWIRED
Supply Licence Conditions span consumer protection, financial resilience and market conduct. This scorecard traffic-lights only the domains this simulation can observe from its own billing, complaints and payment data -- an honest subset of the full SLC set.
FRA Capital RatioEquity / monthly revenue: ≥2x GREEN, 1–2x AMBER, <1x RED. Post-2022 Ofgem FRA requirement.WIRED
Financial Resilience and Additional (Capital) Requirements, introduced after the 2021-22 supplier failure wave. Ofgem's stress test combines market-price risk (VaR) and credit risk against held capital, not revenue alone.
Settlement ReconciliationElexon R1/R2/RF timeline; 90% HH portfolio = GREEN RAG; outstanding pool up to 28 monthsWIRED
Elexon settles half-hourly positions in stages (Initial, R1, R2, RF) over as long as 14 months as metering data firms up. This tracks the gap between initial and final settled positions, not just the final number.
Key Regulatory Findings

WHD / ECO Exemption

Both mandatory only for >150k domestic customers. Portfolio has 2–5 residential customers -- EXEMPT for all years. Growth warning: obligation triggers at ~150k customers.

CCL 2019 Spike

April 2019: electricity CCL +45%, gas CCL +67%. Largest single-year policy cost increase in the dataset. I&C pass-through means this hits customers directly.

FiT Scheme Closure

FiT closed to new applicants April 2019. Board section shows cost falling to NIL from 2020. Smart Export Guarantee replaced it but is not a supplier levy.

FRA Capital Post-2022

Ofgem FRA requires combined market + credit stress. Model: equity > (price VaR + credit stress). Board RAG shows FRA compliance per year 2016–2025.

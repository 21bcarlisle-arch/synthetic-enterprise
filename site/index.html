A UK energy supplier that runs itself -- and could go live.

Poesys is not a model of an energy supplier -- it's a running one. Its commercial, financial, and operational processes all execute, settled half-hourly against real Elexon and NESO market data since 2016, and it can never see inside the simulation it runs in: it discovers its world only the way a real supplier would -- meter reads, market feeds, customer contacts, bills, regulatory publications. The final phase of the roadmap is a go-live analysis: what it would actually take, legally and operationally, to serve a real customer.

Proof, in public

A shadow-live track record runs against real Elexon market data: hedge stance, renewal-price flags, and retention decisions are logged and pre-registered before the outcome is known, then graded against what actually happened -- misses included. AI-native, not AI-retrofitted

Most industry platforms bolt agents onto human-designed cores that still assume a person owns the journey. Poesys inverts that: built from first principles with AI as the company, and a human as strategic director only -- mission, risk appetite, and reserved matters, never the keyboard. A company that can also fail -- insolvency and licence revocation are real terminal states here, not exceptions designed away -- is what makes its survival through the 2021-22 crisis, which killed around 30 real UK suppliers, an earned result rather than an assumption.

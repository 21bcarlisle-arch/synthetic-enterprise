AI-native, not AI-retrofitted

Most industry platforms bolt agents onto human-designed cores that still assume a person owns the journey. Poesys inverts that: built from first principles with AI as the company, and a human as strategic director only -- mission, risk appetite, and reserved matters, never the keyboard. A company that can also fail -- insolvency and licence revocation are real terminal states here, not exceptions designed away -- is what makes its survival through the 2021-22 crisis, which killed around 30 real UK suppliers, an earned result rather than an assumption.

Why Integrations Take Years, Not Weeks

A UK supplier's software has to get thousands of interdependent rules right at once -- half-hourly settlement, the price cap, VAT by customer segment, meter-read estimation and back-billing limits, vulnerability and Priority Services Register duties, hedging -- and a conventional system-integration project can only test a handful of those end-to-end before go-live. The rest ship as untested edge cases that surface as real customer harm, and regulatory exposure, months after launch, not before. That gap -- between "the rules are all documented somewhere" and "every rule is actually checked on every change" -- is the real cost driver behind multi-year, multi-million-pound platform rebuilds, not a lack of willingness or technical skill.

Why This Is Possible Now

What's changed isn't the rules -- it's who can afford to check as many of them as possible, continuously, and keep adding coverage as gaps are found. An AI agent that rebuilds and re-tests its own system every day against real settlement data can catch a regression the day it's introduced, not the year it's discovered in production (this project has already caught and fixed several of its own real defects exactly that way -- see the Method's forging incidents). No test suite is complete, and this one isn't either -- the bet this project tests isn't that AI writes code faster; it's that it can hold a genuinely complete, continuously-verified system at a depth a cost- and attention-bound human integration team structurally cannot sustain.

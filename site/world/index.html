Reluctant Homes — the world is built awkward on purpose

Households are not showrooms. The synthetic population is built to be as awkward as the real one: a boiler from 2011, an inverter that predates wifi, an EV only its own app can talk to, a smart meter that lost signal two years ago, people who ignore, refuse, or agree and then never act.

Most of the energy transition is designed for a fully-instrumented house occupied by someone who enjoys optimising things — and that early adopter has already acted. The carbon that remains is in the other homes. So inertia and reluctance are modelled as the operating conditions, not obstacles to design away: the point is to discover what works for a reluctant household before building anything for it.

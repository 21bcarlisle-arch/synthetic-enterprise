Personalisation — every household its own plan, not a segment average

Their own plan and path. Every household is meant to hold a cost-and-carbon trajectory — what they spend, what they emit, and how both move when the boiler ages, a baby arrives, an EV lands — so every intervention is judged against it: did this household's cost and carbon actually bend, or did we merely send a message?

Timing, not just targeting. Slow-moving traits set the range of what's possible; fast-moving states (a house move, a bill shock, a cold snap, the week before payday) move within it. Habits reset at moments of change, and an intervention timed to one of those windows outperforms the same one sent at random.

Channel and tone. The same message that helps one household lands as harassment in another; the voice is meant to be generated per person, per moment — brisk when they're busy, gentle when they're struggling. Status: the per-household cost-and-carbon trajectory and the £/tCO₂e it feeds are designed, not yet instrumented (see The Proof — what we have not proven).
